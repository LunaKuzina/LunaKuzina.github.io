Unofficial UX/UI Redesign: Returned Warrior

As part of a UI/UX redesign assignment, I analyzed and reworked the UI for Returned Warrior using Figma. The original UI was chaotic and unintuitive, without any clear structure, making navigation frustrating. My goal wasn’t just to improve how the game looked, but also how players interacted with it. Through detailed analysis, iterative design, and feedback, I improved user experience, and increased overall player satisfaction.

Art

I manually extracted and recreated UI elements, icons, and assets using Procreate to maintain quality and ensure stylistic consistency, given the lack of a shared asset library. I selected and refined fonts to align with the game’s pixel aesthetic while prioritizing readability. I also redesigned elements like notification badges and switch menu icons to improve clarity and visual communication without losing the original game’s charm.

Development

I did an in-depth heuristic evaluation of the original UI using Nielsen’s and Hodent’s usability principles to help identify flaws in clarity, consistency, and cognitive load. I restructured layouts, grouped icons by function, simplified navigation, and reduced clutter to improve usability. Using Figma, I iteratively made lo-fi to hi-fi wireframes and i refined functionality based on feedback.

Cats INC.: Card Game

Cats INC. is a lighthearted card game where players take on the roles of cats running a chaotic company. Their goal is to keep the business afloat by managing stocks of yarn, catnip, and dog repellent. Each round, players draw and play cards with various effects and conditions, making decisions that either stabilize or sabotage the company’s resources. The challenge lies in working together just to avoid bankruptcy.

Art

I was responsible for rendering the final designs of our cards. I made the card templates using a pastel color palette, intentionally leaving uneven yet balanced borders to match the game's silly and playful tone. For the first card versions, I handwrote the playable card prompts before transitioning to typed text for the final print. I also fixed style inconsistencies, redesigning our suitcase packaging by adding hand-drawn stickers that better fit with the game’s aesthetic.

Development

I managed the majority of the documentation for the project and was involved in planning to keep the team on track. I often pointed out design flaws during the mechanics and concept development stages, that helped prevent future issues. Throughout, I made sure the visuals and gameplay worked together, supporting both the function and feel of the game.

February 2025 | 1st Year, Block 2

Smirates of the Smaribean Smea

Smirates of the Smaribean Smea is a narrative-driven adventure game where you play as a pirate captain stranded on a remote island after a shipwreck. You search for your missing crew through forests, caves, and ancient fortresses, and uncover that they no longer trust your leadership. The game combines exploration and story to highlight themes of teamwork, trust, and reconnecting with others.

Trailer

Art

I was the main artist for the game, responsible for all pixel art backgrounds and most character designs. I designed distinct colour schemes for each area to ensure they were visually unique and helpful in guiding players through the world. I also made animations that added movement and energy to the game’s scenes, making the overall experience more dynamic and engaging.

Development

I worked with the programmer and second artist to carry the project. Throughout development, I worked with the team to discuss palette creation, refining sprites, animations and backgrounds, and making sure the art style remained consistent and readable. I also contributed to improving dialogue and supported design decisions to ensure the final game was polished.

June 2025 | 1st Year, Block 4

Efteling GO!

Efteling GO! is a story-driven 3D mobile game set in the Efteling theme park. I worked on it with my team, Unity Hell Productions, to develop a game that brings the park’s Sprookjesbos fairy tales to life, allowing players to explore, interact, and restore lost stories by completing quests. By tying gameplay to real-world landmarks, we created an immersive experience that deepens visitors’ connection to the park and its magical world.

Trailer

Art

As the main artist, I was responsible for near all visual aspects of the game’s development. I created the character designs during development, all in-game textures, and UI elements, keeping a cohesive visual style. My work supports the narrative and gameplay by aligning with the Efteling park’s iconic aesthetic.

Development

I worked with my team on all research phases, contributed ideas throughout development, and drew on past experience to inform creative decisions. I worked with different team members at various stages, including integrating 3D models and helping shape the game’s narrative and dialogue.
